Vision · AI for Science

A virtual cell that
can answer counterfactuals.

We believe the next frontier of AI for Science is not predicting labels — it is simulating the causal processes that produce them. Modern single-cell genomics tells us what cells become after a perturbation, but not how they got there. ChronoCell-WM closes that gap.

Given an initial cell state and an intervention program — a gene, a drug, a cytokine, a combination, a schedule — the model simulates the regulatory trajectory step by step, producing endpoint predictions with the mechanistic path that produced them.

“If a cell starts in state S₀ — what happens under A, under B, under A+B, under A→B, or under B→A?”

Why it matters · AI for Drug Development

Drug development is a trajectory problem
solved with snapshot data.

~90%

of drug candidates fail in the clinic — often from resistance, scheduling, or kinetic toxicity that endpoint data cannot reveal.

$2B+

average cost to bring a therapy to market — much of it lost discovering causal mechanisms only after late-stage failures.

104+

combinatorial intervention programs (doses, orders, schedules) per disease — exhaustive wet-lab screening is intractable.

The gap

Static data, dynamic biology

Today's perturbation atlases measure cells at fixed endpoints. The transient regulatory states that drive commitment, resistance, and combination effects are structurally invisible.

The opportunity

Causal trajectories, not labels

A virtual cell that simulates how regulatory states evolve under intervention — not just where they end up — could replace much of the discovery search with in silico hypothesis generation.

The unlock

Time-resolved single-cell data

Recent advances in minute-scale single-cell profiling now make causal regulatory dynamics directly observable — providing the missing supervision for true world-model learning.

The approach · AI for Biology

Four converging research programs.
One causal virtual cell.

ChronoCell-WM is uniquely positioned because it brings together four research programs that rarely live in the same team — each already validated through public technologies, peer-reviewed publications, and open-source releases. Together they cover the full stack required to move biology from endpoint observation to in silico simulation.

  1. I

    Temporal single-cell biology

    Wang Lab · ChronoSeq platform & regulatory inference

    Minute-scale genome-wide single-cell profiling that reveals how regulatory states evolve under intervention — the missing causal substrate that endpoint atlases cannot provide.

  2. II

    Generative world models

    MixLab · Action-conditioned simulation & reasoning

    Frontier-scale world models that represent state, action, and transition — proven on video, language, and reasoning tasks, now extended to cellular dynamics and counterfactual rollouts.

  3. III

    Controllable, plausible reasoning

    Q-Lab · Constrained generation & diverse paths

    Energy-based constraints, diverse-path search, and uncertainty calibration that keep long-horizon rollouts on the biological manifold — making the virtual cell interpretable and falsifiable.

  4. IV

    ML systems at production scale

    PICASSO Lab · Branching workloads & efficient inference

    Production-grade compiler, scheduling, and caching infrastructure validated on the largest open models — the systems substrate that makes counterfactual rollout at biological scale tractable.

The team

Four labs. One full stack.

ChronoCell-WM requires capabilities that rarely live in one team: minute-scale single-cell biology, world modeling at frontier scale, constrained reasoning, and production-grade ML systems. The four collaborating labs at UC San Diego cover every layer — with an existing track record of co-authorship.

I — Temporal single-cell biology

Wei Wang

Professor, Chemistry & Biochemistry · UC San Diego

Wang is an internationally recognized leader in regulatory genomics and single-cell biology. His lab developed ChronoSeq — the technology that pushed single-cell transcriptomics to minute-scale temporal resolution — and has built directed transcription-factor atlases validated by CRISPR perturbation (Nature, 2026). Decades of expertise in regulatory network inference and experimental validation anchor the project's biological foundation.

Direction

From seed dataset
to a discovery engine.

Three phases, each with a public release. Every phase is designed to be falsifiable — we will know, at each step, whether the model is learning real biology or memorizing labels.

  1. Phase I · Foundation

    Establishing the causal substrate

    Public release: seed dataset & first model

    • Generate the first dense-time perturbation atlas
    • Train an initial regulatory-transition world model
    • Validate against known transcription-factor directionality

  2. Phase II · Extension

    Combinations, schedules, and constraints

    Public release: expanded atlas & rollout model

    • Expand coverage of cell contexts and intervention programs
    • Extend to combinatorial and sequential interventions
    • Integrate constrained reasoning & uncertainty calibration

  3. Phase III · Platform

    Open discovery engine

    Public release: full atlas, APIs, benchmarks

    • Release final dataset, model family, and distilled open variants
    • Deploy public APIs, notebooks, tutorials, and leaderboards
    • Prospective wet-lab validation against standard screening

Impact

A force multiplier for predictive biology — open by design.

ChronoCell-WM is built to be released. Every dataset, model, benchmark, and API will be open — lowering the barrier to virtual-cell simulation for labs without large screening infrastructure and unlocking the latent value of every existing endpoint atlas in the field.

For under-resourced institutions and labs in low- and middle-income countries, this means access to causal in silico simulation without multi-million-dollar sequencing budgets. For the field at large, it means a new evaluation standard and a compounding network effect: every new perturbation dataset becomes more causally valuable because the transition operator exists.
